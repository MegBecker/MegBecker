Electronic Student Services

As a student, I worked on campus developing branding, graphics, and UX design for the university's Department of Student Development. One of my main projects was to create a visual system to represent ESS and the software produced there.

Working with developers, I designed a new theme to be used on all of the department's websites, restructuring information and making it easier to navigate with a top-tier and side menu system. Before, only a side menu structured information, making it difficult to find things easily.

As a part of ESS's branding and the carousel banner system of the new theme, banners tie in the visual system on the official website on the university's system. View it in action here.

Branding and icon design for Appalachian's Prevent Suicide campaign. A visual system was needed for promotion and consistency. View it here.

Icons carry the color scheme and add vibrancy and hope to the message, tying the green of the official campaign with the gold of the university.

Internship Inventory is an online database that helps professors manage and stay on top of internships, solving past issues of filing. A logo and corresponding marketing site for the software was produced to share with both faculty and the open-source community.

I coded and designed the marketing site (HTML/CSS/Bootstrap), expanding on the logo to create a branding system; it can be viewed here.

Homestead is the university's housing software. Above is my mockup and branding scheme for the faculty interface, colorfully illustrating students' profiles and capacity of residence halls. The interface was handed off to a developer for production after finalization after a semester of back and forth collaboration with developers.

The student interface is simpler, using an icon set to showcase the main aspects of each residence hall, with all the main information available at a glance, highlighting photos and information.

A line icon style was chosen for simplicity.
