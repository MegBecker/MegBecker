Electronic Student Services

As a student, I worked on campus at Electronic Student Services doing branding, graphics, and front-end development for the university's Department of Student Development. One of my main projects was to brand ESS and the software produced there.

Working with developers, I designed a new theme to be used on all of the department's websites, restructuring information and making it easier to navigate with a top-tier and side menu system.

As a part of ESS's branding and the carousel banner system of the new theme, bright graphics add interest and clarify who ESS is.

Internship Inventory is an online database that helps professors manage and stay on top of internships. Going along with ESS's branding and the colors of the university, a logo and icon set was created, as well as a marketing website for the software. The website is responsive and can be viewed here.


Homestead is housing software the university uses. It is currently undergoing a complete change and being redesigned from the ground up. Above is my mockup and branding scheme for the faculty interface, colorfully illustrating students' profiles and capacity of residence halls.


The student interface is simpler, using an icon set to showcase the main aspects of each residence hall.

Initial mockups for the marketing site that is still in progress. The site uses all the branding colors and illustrations to help the software pop.
